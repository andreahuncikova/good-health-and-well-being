ABOUT

Over the last 15 years, the number of childhood deaths has been cut in half. This proves that it is possible to win the fight against almost every disease. Still, we are spending an astonishing amount of money and resources on treating illnesses that are surprisingly easy to prevent.

The new goal for worldwide Good Health promotes healthy lifestyles, preventive measures, and modern, efficient healthcare for everyone.

400 million

At least 400 million people have no basic healthcare, and 40 percent lack social protection.

2 seconds

Every 2 seconds someone aged 30 to 70 years dies prematurely from noncommunicable diseases - cardiovascular disease, chronic respiratory disease, diabetes or cancer.

1.6 billion

More than 1.6 billion people live in fragile settings where protracted crises, combined with weak national capacity to deliver basic health services, present a significant challenge to global health.

7 million

7 million people die every year from exposure to fine particles in polluted air.

GOALS

PREVENT AND TREAT SUBSTANCE ABUSE

Prevention and treatment of substance abuse, including drug abuse and harmful use of alcohol, must be strengthened.

Harmful use of alcohol, defined according to the national context as alcohol consumption per inhabitant (from 15 years and above) within a calendar year in liters of pure alcohol.

ACHIEVE UNIVERSAL HEALTH COVERAGE

Universal health coverage must be achieved, including protection against financial risks, access to high-quality essential health services, and access to safe and effective quality life-saving medicines and vaccines at an affordable price for all.

IMPLEMENT THE WHO FRAMEWORK CONVENTION ON TOBACCO CONTROL

The implementation of the World Health Organization's Framework Convention on Tobacco Control must be strengthened in all countries where it is relevant.

Age-standardized prevalence of tobacco use among persons aged 15 and over.
